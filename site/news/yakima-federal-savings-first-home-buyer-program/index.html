Yakima Federal Savings Launches First-Time Home Buyer Assistance Program

June 18, 2026

 

Yakima Federal Savings is proud to announce the launch of its new First-Time Home Buyer Assistance Program, designed specifically to help working families in Kennewick, Richland, and Pasco achieve the dream of homeownership. The program, which became available on June 18, 2026, offers reduced down payment requirements, below-market interest rates, and personalized one-on-one financial coaching for qualified applicants. This initiative underscores the association's nearly century-long mission of keeping homeownership accessible to all members of the Tri-Cities community.

Program Features and Eligibility

Under the new program, eligible first-time buyers may qualify for a down payment as low as 3% on conventional home loans, along with interest rates that are up to 0.5% below standard market rates. To qualify, applicants must not have owned a primary residence in the past three years, must have a stable employment history of at least two years, and must complete the Yakima Federal home buyer education course — offered free of charge at all three branch locations or online. Income limits apply and are based on current area median income guidelines for Benton and Franklin counties.

Financial Coaching and Pre-Approval

Every participant in the program receives a complimentary session with a Yakima Federal mortgage specialist who will review their credit profile, help them understand their purchase budget, and outline a realistic path to closing. "We know that buying your first home can feel overwhelming," said Katie Tripp, Assistant Branch Manager at the Richland office. "Our goal is to break down every step and make sure our members never feel alone in the process." Pre-approval letters can typically be issued within 48 hours of a completed application.

Why Local Lending Matters

Unlike large national lenders, Yakima Federal makes all lending decisions locally, with underwriters who understand the Tri-Cities housing market. Loan files are reviewed by staff who live and work in the same communities as their borrowers. This local approach means faster decisions, more flexible consideration of non-traditional credit histories, and a commitment to keeping families in homes even when life circumstances change. The association has originated over $200 million in home loans since its founding.

How to Apply

Prospective buyers can begin the application process by visiting any Yakima Federal branch, calling the mortgage lending department at (509) 248-0430, or accessing the online inquiry form at yakimafederal.com. Staff are available Monday through Friday to answer questions and schedule initial consultations. The program has limited capacity and applicants are encouraged to apply early to secure their spot in the current cohort. Yakima Federal is an equal housing lender and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, disability, or familial status.

Yakima Federal Savings Awards $50,000 in Community Development Grants

May 5, 2026

 

Yakima Federal Savings is pleased to announce the recipients of its 2026 Community Development Grant Program, distributing a total of $50,000 to twelve non-profit organizations and civic projects across Benton and Franklin counties. The grants, awarded on May 5, 2026, support initiatives ranging from affordable housing development and youth financial literacy education to food security programs and neighborhood revitalization efforts. This is the largest single grant cycle in the association's history and reflects Yakima Federal's deepened investment in the long-term wellbeing of the Tri-Cities region.

Grant Recipients and Project Highlights

Among the twelve recipients, Tri-Cities Community Action Committee received $8,000 to expand its emergency rental assistance program, which helped more than 400 households avoid eviction in 2025. The Mid-Columbia Library Foundation received $5,000 to fund a financial literacy workshop series for high school students across Kennewick, Richland, and Pasco school districts. Habitat for Humanity of Benton-Franklin Counties received $10,000 — the largest individual award — to support construction labor and materials on two affordable homes currently underway in Pasco's east side neighborhood. Each grant application was reviewed by a community advisory panel composed of local educators, business owners, and civic leaders.

Philosophy Behind the Grants

"Yakima Federal exists because of this community," said Dan Gaulke, President and CEO. "When we invest in organizations that build stronger families and stronger neighborhoods, we are fulfilling our core purpose." The Community Development Grant Program was established in 2018 with a $10,000 initial pool. Over the past eight years, Yakima Federal has awarded more than $220,000 to community organizations, making it one of the region's most consistent sources of philanthropic support from a financial institution.

Application Process for Future Cycles

Non-profit organizations and community groups interested in applying for the next grant cycle, tentatively scheduled for Spring 2027, may request an application packet from any Yakima Federal branch. Applications will be accepted starting January 2027 and the deadline for submissions is March 31, 2027. Eligible organizations must be 501(c)(3) registered entities serving residents within Benton or Franklin County and must demonstrate a direct benefit to low-to-moderate income populations. Priority consideration is given to projects focused on housing stability, economic mobility, and financial education.

Celebrating Community Partnership

Yakima Federal Savings invites all Tri-Cities residents to learn more about the organizations that received 2026 Community Development Grants and to support their missions through volunteering, donations, and advocacy. A complete list of grant recipients and their funded projects is available at any Yakima Federal branch or on the association's website. The association thanks its members — whose deposits power every loan, grant, and community initiative — for making this work possible year after year.

Yakima Federal Savings Raises CD Rates to Historic Highs for Tri-Cities Savers

July 10, 2026

 

Yakima Federal Savings announced this week that it is raising certificate of deposit (CD) interest rates across all terms, offering some of the most competitive returns available to Tri-Cities residents in years. Effective July 10, 2026, rates on 12-month CDs will climb to 5.10% APY, while 24-month and 36-month terms will offer 5.25% and 5.40% APY respectively. These increases reflect the association's commitment to rewarding loyal savers and helping local families build long-term financial security.

Why CD Rates Are Rising

The rate increases follow a sustained period of elevated federal benchmark rates. Yakima Federal Savings is passing those gains directly to its depositors rather than pocketing the margin. "Our members are the reason we exist," said Dan Gaulke, President and CEO. "When market conditions allow us to pay more, we believe it is our obligation to do so." The new rates are available to both existing and new CD holders starting immediately.

How to Open a CD at Yakima Federal

Opening a CD at Yakima Federal Savings is straightforward. Members can visit any of the three Tri-Cities branch locations — in Pasco, Kennewick (Gage), and Richland — or speak with a financial advisor by phone. Minimum deposit requirements start at $500, making the accounts accessible for a wide range of savers. CDs may be opened as individual or joint accounts, and funds are federally insured up to $250,000 through NCUA.

Comparing Options: CDs vs. Savings Accounts

While standard savings accounts offer flexibility and immediate access to funds, CDs provide a guaranteed fixed return for a predetermined term. For households with a portion of savings they can set aside for 12 months or longer, a CD represents a low-risk strategy to maximize earnings. Yakima Federal advisors encourage members to review their overall savings strategy and consider laddering CDs of different terms to balance liquidity with yield.

Community Impact

Strong deposit growth helps Yakima Federal fund more local mortgage and consumer loans, keeping money circulating within the Tri-Cities economy. Every CD opened at Yakima Federal contributes to the association's capacity to serve first-time home buyers, small businesses, and community organizations throughout the region.
